Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2025-12-19

This Privacy Policy explains how Hockey Pong (“we”, “us”) collects and uses information when you use our web application (accounts, multiplayer game, chat, friends, and match history).

Information We Collect

  • Account data: username, email (if you provide it), profile info, profile picture.
  • Authentication & security: session cookies/tokens; 2FA enabled status (if you use 2FA). 2FA secrets are stored securely.
  • Social & content: friends list, friend requests, chat messages you send.
  • Gameplay data: match history and related game statistics.
  • Technical data: IP address and basic logs needed to operate and secure the service.

How We Use Information

  • To create and manage accounts and sessions.
  • To provide gameplay features (lobbies/matchmaking) and show match history.
  • To deliver chat and social features.
  • To prevent abuse, debug issues, and keep the service secure.

Cookies / Local Storage

We use cookies (or similar storage) to keep you signed in and to make the application work properly. You can clear cookies in your browser, but some features may stop working.

Sharing of Data

We do not sell your personal data. We only share data when necessary to run the service (for example: hosting/infrastructure providers) or if required by law.
Service providers (if applicable): Hosted on local Docker deployment.

Data Retention

We keep account, chat, and match history data for as long as your account remains active, or as needed to operate the service. You can request deletion as described below.
Deletion contact: Contact the team via the project repository issues: https://github.com/PBjr2002/Transcendence.

Your Choices & Rights

  • You can update certain profile details inside the app.
  • You can request account deletion by contacting us at: Contact the team via the project repository issues: https://github.com/PBjr2002/Transcendence.
  • You can disable cookies in your browser (may break login/session features).

Security

We use reasonable measures to protect information (for example, encrypted transport where available and access controls). No system is 100% secure.

Children’s Privacy

The service is not intended for children under the age of 13. If you believe a child has provided data, contact us and we will handle it.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy. We will change the “Last updated” date when we do.

Contact

For privacy questions or deletion requests, contact: Contact the team via the project repository issues: https://github.com/PBjr2002/Transcendence.
